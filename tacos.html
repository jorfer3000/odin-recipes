Tacos

Tacos are a versatile and beloved dish from Mexican cuisine, consisting of a folded or rolled tortilla filled with an array of ingredients. The traditional fillings include seasoned meats such as beef, chicken, or pork, complemented by fresh vegetables, cheese, and various sauces. The beauty of tacos lies in their simplicity and the freedom to customize each one to individual tastes.

Whether you enjoy your tacos with just a sprinkle of cilantro and chopped onion or loaded with salsa, guacamole, and sour cream, the possibilities are endless. This adaptability makes tacos a favorite among both the culinary adventurers and those who prefer the classic street-style taco.

Ingredients

  • 12 corn or flour tortillas
  • 1 pound ground beef or shredded chicken
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • Cilantro to taste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Your choice of salsa, guacamole, and sour cream

Steps

  1. Heat the tortillas in a skillet over medium heat or directly over a flame until they are warm and slightly crispy.
  2. In a separate skillet, cook the ground beef or shredded chicken with salt and pepper until fully cooked.
  3. Add chopped onions to the meat and cook until they are translucent.
  4. Assemble the tacos by placing the cooked meat on each tortilla, followed by tomatoes, lettuce, and cheese.
  5. Add a sprinkle of cilantro and a squeeze of lime over the top for added flavor.
  6. Serve immediately with sides of salsa, guacamole, and sour cream for guests to add to their liking.
