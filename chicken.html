Roasted Chicken

Roasted chicken, a universal comfort food, is cherished across cultures for its tender, juicy meat and crispy, golden skin. This dish is as versatile as it is popular, providing a satisfying centerpiece for a family dinner or a festive occasion. The key to perfect roasted chicken lies in the right blend of seasoning and proper cooking technique, ensuring every bite is infused with flavor and moisture.

The simplicity of a classic roasted chicken allows for countless variations in seasonings and accompaniments, making it a dish that can be reinvented time and again. Whether you prefer a traditional herb rub or something more adventurous, roasted chicken is a reliable and delightful choice that never fails to impress.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (about 4 to 5 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • Fresh herbs (like rosemary and thyme) for stuffing

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit (190 degrees Celsius).
  2. Rinse the chicken under cold water and pat dry with paper towels. This will help the skin get extra crispy.
  3. Rub the olive oil all over the chicken. In a small bowl, mix the salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, and thyme. Sprinkle this seasoning mixture evenly over the chicken.
  4. Stuff the cavity of the chicken with the lemon halves, smashed garlic cloves, and fresh herbs.
  5. Place the chicken in a roasting pan and roast in the preheated oven for about 1 hour and 20 minutes, or until the internal temperature of the chicken reaches 165 degrees Fahrenheit (74 degrees Celsius).
  6. Remove the chicken from the oven and let it rest for at least 10 minutes before carving. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, ensuring the chicken is moist and flavorful.
