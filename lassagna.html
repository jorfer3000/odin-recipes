Lassagna

Lasagna, a beloved staple in Italian cuisine, is a dish that combines the rich flavors of layered pasta, savory meats, and creamy cheeses. Originating from the region of Emilia-Romagna, traditional lasagna is made with layers of flat lasagna noodles, Bolognese sauce (a slow-cooked meat sauce), and béchamel, a white sauce. Each layer is generously sprinkled with Parmigiano-Reggiano, creating a deliciously indulgent meal that's perfect for gatherings and family dinners.

Today, lasagna has evolved into numerous variations that cater to different tastes and dietary preferences. From vegetarian options that include layers of roasted vegetables and ricotta, to contemporary twists like seafood or chicken lasagna, there’s a version for everyone. This versatility makes lasagna not only a comfort food favorite but also a canvas for creativity in the kitchen, inviting cooks to explore new combinations and flavors.

  • 9 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 2 cups Bolognese sauce
  • 1 cup béchamel sauce
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In a skillet, heat the olive oil and cook the ground beef until browned. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions until al dente, then drain and set aside.
  4. Layer the bottom of a baking dish with a layer of Bolognese sauce, then place three lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  5. Spread a portion of the béchamel sauce over the noodles, then add a layer of cooked ground beef and dollops of ricotta cheese.
  6. Sprinkle a layer of mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses, then repeat the layering process until all ingredients are used.
  7. Cover the final layer with remaining cheeses and bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  8. Allow the lasagna to rest for 10 minutes before serving to let it set up for easier slicing.
